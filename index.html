🧠 About ADHD

Understanding Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder helps families, teachers, and students support one another with empathy and evidence-based strategies.

What is ADHD?

ADHD (Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder) is a neurodevelopmental condition characterised by persistent patterns of inattention, hyperactivity, and impulsivity that interfere with functioning or development. It affects how the brain manages attention, activity, and self-control — not a lack of intelligence or effort.

Three Core Symptom Domains

Inattention

Difficulty sustaining focus, following through on tasks, organising activities, and being easily distracted. May appear as careless mistakes or "daydreaming."

Hyperactivity

Excessive movement, fidgeting, difficulty staying seated, and feeling constantly "on the go." More visible in younger children but persists in subtler forms in teens.

Impulsivity

Acting without thinking — blurting answers, interrupting, difficulty waiting turns. Can lead to social challenges and impulsive decisions.

Executive Functions

ADHD significantly affects executive functions — the brain's "management system":

Planning

Breaking tasks into steps and anticipating what's needed ahead of time.

Working Memory

Holding information in mind while completing multi-step activities.

Task Initiation

Getting started on tasks without excessive procrastination or avoidance.

Time Management

Estimating how long tasks take and meeting deadlines consistently.

Morning Routine Challenges

Many children with ADHD struggle with morning routines due to difficulty with task initiation, time blindness, and working memory. Simple tools like visual checklists, preparing the night before, and consistent wake-up routines can make a significant difference.
